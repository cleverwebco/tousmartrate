Understanding TOU

When You Use Power
Is Just as Important as How Much

Wake Electric's TOU rate divides every day into two pricing windows. The on-peak rate is 3× higher than off-peak. The key insight: on-peak windows are only 2 hours long. If your household's heaviest usage naturally falls outside those windows — you win. I look at your actual data to find out.

The Basics

What Makes TOU
Different from a Flat Rate?

🕐

The Clock Matters

Wake Electric's TOU rate charges 24.0¢/kWh during on-peak hours and just 8.0¢/kWh the rest of the time. Compare that to the standard flat rate of 13.0¢/kWh for every hour. If your heavy usage happens off-peak, you come out ahead — often significantly.

📅

Two Short Windows, All Year

In winter (Nov–Mar), on-peak is 6–8 AM. In summer (Apr–Oct), it's 4–6 PM. That's just two hours a day. Households whose natural routines keep them off-peak during those windows often see immediate savings without changing a thing.

📊

Your Interval Data Is the Key

Your smart meter records usage every 15–60 minutes. That granular history is what I use to model your exact bill under each rate — not estimates, not averages, but your actual usage recalculated against each pricing schedule.

🔄

Not Everyone Benefits — I'll Tell You Honestly

TOU isn't a universal win. If your household runs heavy loads during peak windows, you may genuinely be better off on the flat rate. I run the real numbers and give you a straight answer either way — even if that answer is "stay where you are."
